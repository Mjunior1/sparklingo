# SparkLingo Mascot Direction

## Goal
The mascot needs to be the emotional center of the product, not a decorative icon.
It should feel:

- playful
- memorable
- premium
- expressive
- slightly mischievous
- educational without looking academic

## Visual Rules

- Big readable eyes
- Strong silhouette, recognizable even in small size
- Soft glossy materials
- Rounded toy-like body
- Exaggerated facial expressions
- Clear accent prop linked to learning
- Lavender and golden-yellow as core brand anchors

## Recommended Concept

### Concept A: Spark
A round, bouncy purple creature with floating ears/horns and a giant pencil as signature prop.

Why this is the best fit:

- easiest to turn into a brand icon
- compact silhouette works in sidebar, hero, badges, and loading states
- already close enough to the current visual language to evolve without breaking the product
- can support many expressions and costumes

Personality:

- cheerful
- quick
- curious
- slightly chaotic in a cute way

## Alternative Concepts

### Concept B: Luna Fox
A small fox-pilot learner with aviator goggles, scarf, and satchel.

Pros:

- stronger narrative world
- more emotional warmth

Cons:

- more detailed, harder to read at very small sizes
- less app-icon friendly

### Concept C: Byte Bird
A smart little bird with oversized headphones, tiny backpack, and microphone charm.

Pros:

- great for speaking/listening products
- naturally dynamic and expressive

Cons:

- less universal than Spark
- can skew too much toward audio instead of full learning journey

## Prompt Pattern

Use these constraints for all generations:

- children educational game mascot
- premium 3d cartoon render
- bold silhouette
- glossy soft materials
- vibrant but clean palette
- expressive face
- white or soft lavender background
- no text
- centered composition
- full character visible

## What To Generate

1. Spark: main recommended direction
